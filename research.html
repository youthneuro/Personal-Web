Optimizing Memory via Real-Time Monitoring of Neural Data

Authors: R Cho, J Kragel

Recognition: 1st Place at the 62nd National Junior Science and Humanities Symposium, Stanford Virtual Neuroscience Forum Oral Presentation, Regional Science Fair Winner (time-conflicts so couldn't attend state), 2024

Description: Analyzed intracranial neural data from 38 patients. Used natural language processing, machine learning, and signal processing to develop a dual-algorithm system that can target deep brain stimulation at an accuracy of 90% to improve memory. Discovered that the LTC improved semantic organization and the brain's functional network overall, while the hippocampus improved temporal organization, a novel finding.

Investigating Brain Activity Patterns During Learning Tasks

Authors: R Cho, M Zaman, KT Cho, J Hwang

Publication: International Journal of Information Technology (Springer Nature), 2024

Citations: 4

Description: Collected neural data from 20 students at Northern Illinois University using EEG sensors. Used machine learning and signal processing to understand how the frontal and temporoparietal lobes contribute to cognitive processing, finding the frontal lobe to be the most critical.

EEG Understanding of Neurodevelopmental Disorders

Authors: R Cho

Recognition: $18k Scholarship, State Science Fair Winner, 2023

Description: Collected neural data from family members solving math problems or listening to Mozart using a homemade EEG setup. Implemented noise removal, multiple analysis domains, and machine learning to create a Flask-based web app that connected to EEG signals via Bluetooth for attention pattern analysis, storing results in Google Firebase.

Differentiating Hand Gestures from Forearm Muscle Activity Using Machine Learning

Authors: R Cho, S Puli, J Hwang

Publication: International Journal of Occupational Safety and Ergonomics (Taylor & Francis), 2024

Description: Collected EMG data from 10 students at Northern Illinois University. Explored different temporal windows, spectral analysis approaches, and machine learning algorithms to develop a musculoskeletal-friendly approach for VR applications.

Authors: R Cho, M Zaman, KT Cho, J Hwang
